Happy Birthday, Riddhi

(to my Ryukkkk)

Riddhi—my Ryukkkk—happy birthday. If I had to explain you, I’d say you’re the softest kind of magic: the kind that doesn’t shout, it stays. You make ordinary moments feel like memories I want to keep. I love the way you exist—your smile that fixes my mood, your eyes that feel like home, your little habits that I never want to forget, and the calm you bring to my chaos without even trying. I don’t only want you in my “good” times; I want you in my every life—every version of tomorrow, every sunrise, every small celebration, every quiet night. I want to learn you again and again, choose you again and again, and hold your hand through everything—success, struggle, distance, closeness, silly laughter, and the deep, peaceful silence that feels safe with you. You’re not just a wish I make—you’re the reason I believe in wishes. So today, I’m wishing you a year that treats you gently: full of warmth, surprises, and reasons to smile. And I’m promising you this—my heart is yours, in this life and in every life after.

Always yours,

— from me
